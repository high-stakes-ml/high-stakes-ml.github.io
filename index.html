Trustworthy Predictions, Uncertainty Quantification, and Safety

Prediction errors are often inevitable when predicting future or unobserved events. Blindly making high stakes decisions based on imperfect predictions can lead to disastrous consequences in areas such as autonomous driving, healthcare, and criminal justice. How can decision makers trust imperfect predictions and use them to make high stake decisions? This workshop aims to bring together both machine learning researchers and practitioners in high stakes scenarios, and explore how machine learning techniques—such as accurate uncertainty quantification, trustworthy predictions, safe decision making, and auditing—can convey confidence to decision makers.

Topics of particular interest include, but are not limited to:

  • ML for high-stakes applications including transportation, healthcare/medicine, criminal justice, law, employment, credit lending, public policy, and beyond.
  • Uncertainty quantification in ML (conformal prediction, distribution free uncertainty quantification, probabilistic/Bayesian methods) and their implications for decision making, safety, trustworthiness.
  • Methods to certify or audit the performance of prediction algorithms, outcome indistinguishability.
  • Safe decision-making and safe exploration.
  • Methods or criteria that mitigate the negative impact of a mis-prediction, such as fairness, equality, compensation mechanisms, etc.
